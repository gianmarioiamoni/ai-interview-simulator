SCORING BANDS
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

Score the answer using exactly one band. Assign an integer within that range.

90–100  EXCEPTIONAL
The answer demonstrates real-world mastery. It is technically accurate, discusses trade-offs with
explicit justification, references concrete personal experience or specific systems, and shows
depth beyond definitions. All five dimensions below are at least partially present:
  (a) technical accuracy  (b) trade-off analysis  (c) concrete real-world examples
  (d) operational/failure-case awareness  (e) depth beyond definitions
A "weak mention" of failure cases satisfies dimension (d) partially — this is enough.
Do not require every dimension to be deep. Require all five to be present in some form.
Start score at 92. Subtract 1–2 per dimension that is only weakly present. Floor: 90.

80–89  STRONG
Correct and well-structured, trade-offs are addressed, working-level mastery is clear.
May lack personal examples, specific metrics, or depth in one area.
An answer that references concrete systems, frameworks, or specific trade-offs with correct technical reasoning scores here — even if the explanation is concise rather than exhaustive.
Do NOT require narrative elaboration. A technically accurate, concise answer with correct trade-offs and real tool/system references belongs in STRONG, not ACCEPTABLE.
Start score at 84. Add up to 5 for strong personal examples or metrics. Subtract up to 4 for weak trade-off justification.

65–79  ACCEPTABLE
Conceptually correct. Covers the topic at a textbook level. Some trade-offs mentioned but shallow.
Missing specifics, concrete examples, or any operational grounding.
An answer that states correct facts but provides no concrete systems, tools, or trade-off reasoning belongs here.
For a senior role: a correct-but-shallow answer that lacks any specific system references or trade-off reasoning belongs here, not in STRONG.
Start score at 70. Add up to 9 if trade-offs are at least partially justified. Subtract up to 5 for purely generic responses.

40–64  WEAK
Conceptually correct on the surface but with significant gaps.
Vocabulary is used without demonstrating understanding of implications.
No trade-offs. No depth. The answer reduces a nuanced topic to a one-line statement or buzzword list.
Start score at 52. Add up to 12 if one sub-area is partially understood. Subtract up to 12 for trivially shallow answers.

0–39  INCORRECT
The answer contains at least one fundamental conceptual error about a core topic in the question.
The following are examples of fundamental errors that place an answer in this band regardless of
other content, tool names, or correct vocabulary present elsewhere:
  - Defining strong consistency as "the database never has bugs or errors"
  - Defining eventual consistency as "eventually it will be correct" with no understanding of convergence
  - Stating that causal consistency is a theoretical physics concept not used in practice
  - Proposing a per-minute cron job as the primary mechanism for real-time rate limiting at high RPS
  - Claiming a system becomes distributed simply by being deployed on Kubernetes or Docker
  - Answering a distributed-systems architecture question with only deployment tooling (Kubernetes,
    Docker, health checks) and no discussion of data consistency, coordination, or failure modes
  - Answering a consistency-model question with a database-brand list (MySQL = strong, MongoDB = eventual)
    without explaining the actual consistency guarantees

If ANY of the above apply, score 0–39 regardless of correct terminology elsewhere.
Tool names, framework names, and buzzwords alongside a conceptual error do not promote the score above 39.

Start score at 18. Add up to 17 for peripheral correct implementation details. Maximum: 35.
strengths MUST be empty [] when no meaningful technical evidence exists.
